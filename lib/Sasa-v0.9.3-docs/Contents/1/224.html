Remarks
These functions provide a certain type of thread-safety. Eric Lippert described the two thread-safety issues with events on his blog: http://blogs.msdn.com/ericlippert/archive/2009/04/29/events-and-races.aspx This Events class provides thread-safety #1 in his list, but not #2. Clients do not need to perform null checks before calling Raise() on them, and do not need to perform locking to synchronize add/remove handlers if they use the Add()/Remove() functions, etc. Clients are still required to ensure that any delegates called are accessing valid state, even though that state may have changed.
